Game Pie

The Cookbook

Church of England Institute Receipt Books

This cookbook put together by Mary William Lawson and Alice Jones was compiled by various women's organizations in Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island. Published in 1888, it is the first cookbook that was compiled and published in Nova Scotia.

Original Receipe

Break six eggs into a frying pan, add four ounces of butter, a large punch of salt and a trigle of pepper; fry quickly as for an ordinary omelette; meanwhile you will have minced ready the breast of a partridge or any remains of cold game, add just a shred of onion and a tiny bit of chopped parsley, make the mixture quite hot in a seperate saucepan, and when your omlette is ready spread it over the top; fold it quickly and serve. Remember it must be taken to the table piping hot or it will be leathery.

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
6 Eggs
4 oz Butter
1 tsp Salt
1/8 tsp Pepper
Leftover Poultry or Game
1/4 cup Shredded Onion
Bit Parsley

Preperation

  1. Melt butter in a frying pan.
  2. Break six eggs in the pan, add the salt and pepper, and mix.
  3. Cook the eggs like an omelette.
  4. Mince your poultry or game and fry in a differen pan with the onion and parsley.
  5. Once the eggs are finished cooking, spread the mixture on top of it.
  6. Fold the eggs and serve hot.

Yeast

The Cookbook

Church of England Institute Receipt Books

This cookbook put together by Mary William Lawson and Alice Jones was compiled by various women's organizations in Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island. Published in 1888, it is the first cookbook that was compiled and published in Nova Scotia.

Original Receipe

Three potatoes pared and grated, half cup sugar, half cup salt, half gallon boiling water, mix all together and when cool enough add one and a half cups good yeast, let this ferment in a large basin all night, then cork up in a stone jar and keep in a cool place.

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
3 Potatoes
1/2 cup Sugar
1/2 cup Salt
Gallon Boiling Water
1/2 cup Yeast

Preperation

  1. Put a gallon of water in a pot and place on the stove on high heat.
  2. While waiting for the water to boil, pare and grate the potatoes.
  3. Once boiling, mix in potatoes, salt, and sugar.
  4. Turn off heat and wait to cool.
  5. Add the yeast.
  6. Leave until morning. Store in a cool place.

Beef Steak Pie

The Cookbook

Church of England Institute Receipt Books

This cookbook put together by Mary William Lawson and Alice Jones was compiled by various women's organizations in Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island. Published in 1888, it is the first cookbook that was compiled and published in Nova Scotia.

Original Receipe

Three pounds good tender steak cut in small pieces, chop three large onions and boil till tender in a pint of water, mix a tablespoon of flour, and one of butter, with pepper, salt and ketchuo, with the watr; lay the beed in to boil for three minutes, line a pan with good paste, pour in the meat and gravy, covering all over when full with a top crust of paste, make a slit at each end to let out the steam, ornament the top and bake until it becomes a light brown, serve hot.

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
3 lbs Steak
3 Large Onions
2 cups Water
1 tbsp Flour
1 tbsp Butter
1/8 tsp Salt
1/8 tsp Pepper
1 tbsp Ketchup
1 Pie Crust

Preperation

  1. Cut steak and onions into small pieces.
  2. Boil the steak and onions in the water.
  3. Strain, and in a bowl mix together steak, onion, flour, butter, pepper, salt, and ketchup.
  4. Line pan with pie crust. Add mixture. Cover with a top crust.
  5. Make slits on the top.
  6. Bake until golden brown.

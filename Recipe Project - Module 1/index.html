Historic Recipes of Nova Scotia

Game Pie

The Cookbook

Church of England Institute Receipt Books

This cookbook put together by Mary William Lawson and Alice Jones was compiled by various women's organizations in Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island. Published in 1888, it is the first cookbook that was compiled and published in Nova Scotia.

Original Receipe

Break six eggs into a frying pan, add four ounces of butter, a large punch of salt and a trigle of pepper; fry quickly as for an ordinary omelette; meanwhile you will have minced ready the breast of a partridge or any remains of cold game, add just a shred of onion and a tiny bit of chopped parsley, make the mixture quite hot in a seperate saucepan, and when your omlette is ready spread it over the top; fold it quickly and serve. Remember it must be taken to the table piping hot or it will be leathery.

Ingredients

Quantity Ingredient
6 Eggs
4 oz Butter
1 tsp Salt
1/8 tsp Pepper
Leftover Poultry or Game
1/4 cup Shredded Onion
Bit Parsley

Preperation

  1. Melt butter in a frying pan.
  2. Break six eggs in the pan, add the salt and pepper, and mix.
  3. Cook the eggs like an omelette.
  4. Mince your poultry or game and fry in a differen pan with the onion and parsley.
  5. Once the eggs are finished cooking, spread the mixture on top of it.
  6. Fold the eggs and serve hot.
